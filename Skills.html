HOW TO PLAY

RULES AND ETTIQUETE

Please Refer to the labelled squash court found further below for court-specific vocabulary


    Materials:
  1. A 1-or-2-dot ball, court-safe shoes (see 'credits' for more information),
    goggles and a racket are all you will need!

    2. Before a game:
  2. It is customary to warm up before a match - this typically lasts around 5 minutes
  3. It is considered proper ettiquete to hit no more than two practice shots to yourself
    before passing to your opponent
  4. Once both you and your opponent are ready, spin the racket to see who serves!
    If you are spinning, ask your opponent to pick a side of the racket.

    5. During a game:
  5. Serve inside the service box, on the side opposite of your opponent's backhand.
    The serve must travel cross-court,reach above the service line,
    and pass the short line.
  6. Return the ball from behind the service box
  7. Get back to the T
  8. A point can be scored regardless of whether you are serving or returning
  9. One point will be scored if the ball is hit out, does not reach the front wall, or bounces twice.
    In professional games, one must respect a judge's call on whether or not a ball is out/has bounced twice
  10. The ball CAN hit side walls and back walls, but not the roof!
  11. If your opponent is in the way of the ball, call a LET. This forces a replay if accepted by the judge.
  12. Occasionally, the judge will advance the LET to a STROKE. This happens when you 100% would have been able to hit the ball, and causes the play to be YOUR POINT
  13. Games are typically played as best 2-of-3 to 11, or best 3-of-5 to 21 for more advanced levels
  14. LINE is OUT!
  15. Call the score after every point
  16. Take a short break after every game
  17. All 'out' lines on the court below are out!

    18. After the game:
  18. Shake hands with your opponent, and tell them good game, regardless of who won or lost.
  19. Exit the court, and shake hands with the referee, thanking them for their time
